👔 Measuring an existing shirt
Find a shirt in your wardrobe that fits you the way you like. Lay it flat on a table, button it up fully, and measure each part. The measurements from your shirt are used directly by our tailor to replicate that exact fit.
📹 Watch how to do it — step by step
⚠️ Important: Pick a shirt that actually fits you well — the one you reach for most often. If you measure a shirt that's too big or small, your new shirt will have the same problem.
Before you start
1
Pick the right shirt
Choose a well-fitting shirt — ideally a formal or casual shirt similar to what you want to order. Button it up completely including the collar.
2
Lay it flat with no wrinkles
Place the shirt face-up on a flat surface — bed, table or floor. Smooth out all folds. The shirt should lie completely flat.
💡 A tailor's measuring tape works best — available at any stationery shop for ₹15-20
MeasurementHow to measure on the shirt
NeckUnbutton the collar, lay flat. Measure from center of button to end of buttonhole.
ChestMeasure across the chest from armhole seam to armhole seam. Multiply by 2.
WaistMeasure across the shirt about 18cm below the armhole. Multiply by 2.
HipMeasure across the bottom hem from side seam to side seam. Multiply by 2.
ShoulderMeasure from one shoulder seam point to the other across the back yoke.
Sleeve lengthFrom the shoulder seam point, measure along the sleeve to the end of the cuff.
BicepMeasure across the sleeve 3cm below where it joins the shirt body. Multiply by 2.
Wrist / CuffMeasure across the open cuff from edge to edge. Multiply by 2.
Shirt lengthFrom the centre back of the collar down to the bottom hem of the shirt.
✅ Double check before submitting: Go through each measurement twice before entering. A small error in chest or shoulder can significantly affect fit.
✂️ Getting measured by a local tailor
The most accurate method and honestly the easiest. Just walk into any tailor shop near you — the one in your neighbourhood market, a darzi, or a shop that does alterations. Most will measure you in under 5 minutes, free of charge.
🔍 Finding a tailor
Any local tailor works. You don't need a specialist — just walk in and ask. They do this all day.
💬 What to say
"Bhai, mujhe shirt ke measurements chahiye — neck, chest, waist, hip, shoulder, sleeve, bicep, wrist aur shirt length." Show them this page if needed.
📐 Ask for inches
Say "inches mein chahiye" — our system uses inches. If they give centimetres, divide each by 2.54 to convert.
💰 Will they charge?
Most won't charge for just measurements. Some may ask ₹20-50. Either way it's worth it for a perfect fit.
Step by step
1
Show the tailor this measurement list
Ask them to take exactly these 9 measurements: Neck, Chest, Waist, Hip, Shoulder, Sleeve Length, Bicep, Wrist, Shirt Length. Screenshot this page and show it to them.
2
Tell them it's for a shirt — not a suit
Shirt measurements are taken differently from suit measurements. Make sure they know so they use the correct technique.
3
Note down each measurement
Write down each measurement as the tailor calls it out, or take a photo of their slip. Then enter these numbers when you checkout on whySML.
4
Tell them your fit preference
Let the tailor know if you prefer slim fit, regular fit, or relaxed. They can note this for context. Add the same preference in your order notes on whySML.

📏 Measuring yourself at home
You'll need a flexible measuring tape (available at any fabric or stationery shop for ₹15-20). Having a friend or family member help makes this much easier and more accurate — especially for chest and shoulder.
📹 Watch how to measure yourself — step by step
⚠️ Before you start: Wear a thin fitted t-shirt or vest. Stand straight with arms relaxed. Don't suck in your stomach — measure normally. Keep the tape snug but not tight.
MeasurementHow to measure on your bodyTypical range
1. Neck Wrap tape around the base of your neck just below the Adam's apple. Keep one finger between tape and neck for comfort room. 14"–18"
2. Chest Wrap tape around the fullest part of your chest, just under your arms. Keep tape parallel to floor. Breathe normally. A friend helps here. 34"–52"
3. Waist Around your natural waist — where you'd wear a belt, about 2-3 inches above belly button. Stand relaxed, don't hold your breath. 28"–48"
4. Hip Around the widest part of your hips and seat, about 8-9 inches below your natural waist. Keep tape parallel to floor. 34"–54"
5. Shoulder From the edge of one shoulder to the other, straight across your upper back. The shoulder point is where your shoulder meets your arm. Best done by a friend. 16"–22"
6. Sleeve Bend your arm slightly. Start from the shoulder point, go over the elbow, to your wrist bone. The bent elbow method gives the most accurate result. 22"–36"
7. Bicep Flex your arm slightly — not hard, just natural. Wrap tape around the fullest part of your upper arm. Should be snug but not tight. 12"–20"
8. Wrist Around your wrist just below the wrist bone, where a watch would sit. Keep snug but comfortable. 6"–9"
9. Shirt length Stand straight. From top of shoulder near neck, measure straight down to where you want the shirt to end. For tucked shirts, measure to mid-hip. 26"–34"
👥 Better with a friend: Chest, shoulder and sleeve are difficult to measure accurately alone. Ask someone to hold the tape — it only takes 5 minutes and makes a big difference in accuracy.

Tips that apply to all methods
✅ Always measure twice
Check each measurement twice before entering. A small error is the most common reason for fit issues and easily avoidable.
📐 Use inches
Our system uses inches. If you get centimetres, divide each by 2.54 to convert. Or just ask the tailor to measure in inches.
💾 Saved forever
Once entered, your measurements are saved to your profile. Every future shirt order uses the same measurements automatically.
