Do it yourself
Measuring an existing shirt
Find a shirt in your wardrobe that fits you the way you like — not too tight, not too loose. Lay it flat on a table or bed, button it up, and measure each part as shown below. The measurements from your shirt will be used directly by our tailor.
⚠️ Important: Make sure the shirt you pick actually fits you well. If you measure a shirt that's too big or too small, your new shirt will have the same problem. When in doubt, pick the shirt you reach for most often.
1
Lay the shirt flat, face up, fully buttoned
Place on a flat surface — table, bed, or floor. Smooth out all wrinkles. Button all buttons including the collar. The shirt should be lying completely flat with no folds.
2
Use a flexible measuring tape
A tailor's tape measure works best. If you don't have one, a regular ruler and a piece of string works too — measure the string after.
💡 Available at any stationery or fabric shop for ₹10-20
Measurement How to measure on the shirt Why it matters
Neck Unbutton the collar. Lay it flat. Measure from the center of the collar button to the far end of the buttonhole. Determines how the collar fits around your neck
Chest Measure straight across the chest, from one armhole seam to the other. Then multiply by 2. How the shirt fits across your chest and back
Waist Measure straight across the shirt about 18cm below the armhole seam. Then multiply by 2. How fitted or relaxed the shirt is at the stomach
Hip Measure straight across the bottom of the shirt hem from side seam to side seam. Then multiply by 2. Important for longer shirts worn untucked
Shoulder Measure from one shoulder seam point to the other across the back yoke of the shirt. Most critical measurement — wrong shoulder makes shirt look completely off
Sleeve Length From the shoulder seam point, measure along the sleeve to the end of the cuff. How long the sleeves are
Bicep Measure across the sleeve about 3cm below where it joins the shirt body. Then multiply by 2. Ensures sleeve isn't tight around the upper arm
Wrist / Cuff Measure across the open cuff from edge to edge. Then multiply by 2. Ensures the cuff fastens comfortably
Shirt Length Measure from the centre back of the collar down to the bottom hem of the shirt. Whether the shirt can be tucked in comfortably
✅
Double check before submitting: Go through each measurement twice. A small error in chest or shoulder measurement can significantly affect the fit. It takes 2 minutes and saves a lot of back and forth.
Getting measured by a local tailor
This is the most accurate method and honestly the easiest — just walk into any tailor shop near you. Most tailors will measure you in under 5 minutes, usually at no charge. You don't need to buy anything from them.
🔍 Finding a tailor near you
Any local tailor shop works — the one in your neighbourhood market, a darzi, or even a shop that does alterations. You don't need a specialist. Just walk in and ask.
💬 What to say
"Bhai, mujhe shirt ke measurements chahiye — neck, chest, waist, hip, shoulder, sleeve, bicep, wrist aur shirt length." Show them this page if needed.
📝 How to record
Ask the tailor to call out each measurement while you note it down on your phone. Or take a photo of their measurement slip if they write it down.
💰 Will they charge?
Most tailors won't charge for just measurements. Some may ask ₹20-50. Either way it's worth it for a perfectly fitting shirt.
1
Show the tailor this measurement list
Ask them to take these 9 measurements: Neck, Chest, Waist, Hip, Shoulder, Sleeve Length, Bicep, Wrist, Shirt Length. You can screenshot this page and show it to them.
2
Tell them it's for a shirt — not a suit
Shirt measurements are taken slightly differently from suit measurements. Make sure they know it's for a shirt so they use the right technique.
3
Note down each measurement in inches
Our system uses inches. If the tailor gives measurements in centimetres, divide each by 2.54 to convert. Or ask them to measure directly in inches.
💡 Simply say "inches mein chahiye" if they measure in cm
4
Enter the measurements when you order
Once you've placed shirts in your cart and proceed to checkout, you'll be asked to enter these measurements. They're saved to your profile permanently — you'll never need to enter them again.
🎯
Pro tip: Tell the tailor how you like your shirts to fit — slim fit, regular fit, or relaxed. They can adjust the measurements slightly based on your preference. Then add a note in your order telling us your preference too.
Measuring yourself at home
You'll need a flexible measuring tape (available at any fabric or stationery shop for ₹10-20). Having a friend help makes this much easier and more accurate — especially for chest and shoulder measurements.
⚠️ Before you start: Wear a thin, fitted t-shirt or vest. Don't measure over thick clothing. Stand straight with your arms relaxed at your sides. Don't hold your breath or suck in your stomach — measure normally.
1. Neck
Wrap the tape around the base of your neck — where your shirt collar sits, just below your Adam's apple. Keep one finger between the tape and your neck for comfort room. The tape should feel snug but not tight.
Typical range:
14" – 18"
2. Chest
Wrap the tape around the fullest part of your chest, just under your arms and across your shoulder blades. Keep the tape parallel to the ground. Breathe normally — don't expand your chest. A friend helps here.
Typical range:
34" – 52"
3. Waist
Wrap the tape around your natural waist — roughly where you'd wear a belt, or about 2-3 inches above your belly button. Stand relaxed, don't hold your breath. This is your shirt waist, not your trouser waist.
Typical range:
28" – 48"
4. Hip
Wrap the tape around the widest part of your hips and seat, usually about 8-9 inches below your natural waist. Keep the tape parallel to the ground. Important if you wear shirts untucked.
Typical range:
34" – 54"
5. Shoulder
Measure from the edge of one shoulder to the edge of the other, straight across your upper back. The shoulder point is where your shoulder meets your arm — you can feel a slight ridge. Best done by a friend.
Typical range:
16" – 22"
6. Sleeve Length
Bend your arm slightly at the elbow. Start from the shoulder point, go over the elbow, and measure to your wrist bone. The bent elbow method gives a more accurate sleeve length than measuring with a straight arm.
Typical range:
22" – 36"
7. Bicep
Flex your arm slightly and wrap the tape around the fullest part of your upper arm. Don't flex hard — just a natural, relaxed flex. The tape should be snug but you should be able to slip a finger underneath.
Typical range:
12" – 20"
8. Wrist
Wrap the tape around your wrist just below the wrist bone, where a watch would sit. Keep the tape snug but comfortable. This determines your cuff size.
Typical range:
6" – 9"
9. Shirt Length
Stand straight. Place the tape at the top of your shoulder, near the base of your neck, and measure straight down to where you want the shirt to end. For a shirt you'll tuck in, measure to about mid-hip. For untucked, measure to where you want the hem.
Typical range:
26" – 34"
👥
Better with a friend: Chest, shoulder and sleeve measurements are difficult to do accurately alone. Ask a family member or friend to hold the tape for you — it makes a significant difference in accuracy. It only takes 5 minutes.
📱 No measuring tape? Visit any nearby tailor and ask them to measure you (Option 2 above). It's free, takes 5 minutes, and is more accurate than measuring yourself.
Tips for all methods
✅ Measure twice
Always check each measurement twice before entering it. A small error is the most common reason for fit issues.
📐 Use inches
Our system uses inches. If you have centimetres, divide by 2.54 to convert. Most Indian tailors can measure in both.
💾 Saved forever
Once you enter your measurements, they're saved to your profile. Every future order uses the same measurements — you never enter them again.
🔧 Free alterations
If your shirt doesn't fit right, we'll alter it for free. Just contact us within 7 days of delivery and we'll collect and fix it.
